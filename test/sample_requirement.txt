Project Brief — Internal Knowledge Assistant (RAG System)
Submitted by: Rajiv Menon, Head of Engineering
Date: Q2 2026

Overview
We need an internal AI assistant that can answer questions from our company's private knowledge base —
including engineering docs, HR policies, product specs, runbooks, and past incident reports.
Currently engineers waste 2–3 hours a day searching Confluence, Notion, and Slack for answers.
We want a chat interface where employees just ask a question and get an accurate, sourced answer.

Core Requirements
- Ingest documents from multiple sources: Confluence, Notion, Google Drive, PDF uploads, and Slack channels
- Chunk, embed, and store documents in a vector database (we are evaluating Pinecone and Weaviate)
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline: retrieve top-k relevant chunks, pass to LLM, return grounded answer with source citations
- Chat interface accessible via web app and Slack bot
- Users must only see documents they are authorised to access (role-based access control on retrieval)
- Admin panel: manage data sources, trigger re-indexing, view usage analytics
- Answer quality: responses must cite the exact source document and page/section
- Feedback loop: users can thumbs up / thumbs down answers to improve retrieval over time

Technical Notes
- LLM: we are open to GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 — whichever gives better grounding
- Embedding model: text-embedding-3-large or equivalent
- Vector store must support metadata filtering for RBAC
- Expected document corpus: ~50,000 documents at launch, growing 20% per year
- Latency target: answers in under 5 seconds for 95th percentile queries
- On-premise deployment preferred due to data sensitivity (no data leaves our VPC)

What Success Looks Like
- 70% reduction in time employees spend searching for information
- Answer accuracy above 85% on internal QA benchmark (we will build this)
- Adoption: 80% of engineering team using it weekly within 3 months of launch
- Zero incidents of unauthorised document access

Open Questions
- How do we handle documents that are updated frequently — what is the re-indexing strategy?
- How do we deal with conflicting information across documents (old runbook vs new runbook)?
- Should we fine-tune the embedding model on our domain or use off-the-shelf?
- What is the fallback when retrieval confidence is low — should the assistant say "I don't know"?

Team & Timeline
- 3 backend engineers, 1 ML engineer, 1 frontend engineer
- MVP (Confluence + PDF ingestion + basic chat): 8 weeks
- Full feature set with RBAC and Slack bot: 16 weeks
- Budget: $120,000
